# White Wine Quality Exploratory Data Analysis

### Introduction 

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis on the White Wine Quality dataset to explore how different chemical properties influence wine quality ratings.

White wines have different characteristics compared to red wines, particularly in terms of sugar content and acidity levels. This analysis aims to explore these characteristics and understand the factors influencing wine quality.

## Dataset Overview 

The white wine dataset contains:

* 4898 observations
* 12 numerical features

This dataset is significantly larger than the red wine dataset, providing a broader representation of wine samples.

## Quality Distribution

Most white wines in the dataset fall within quality ratings between 6 and 7.

## Observation:

Compared to red wines, white wines appear to have slightly higher average quality ratings.

## Feature Distribution

### Residual Sugar

White wines show significantly higher residual sugar levels compared to red wines.

### Insight:

This suggests that white wines tend to be sweeter.

### Alcohol

Alcohol values range between 8% and 14%, similar to the red wine dataset.

## Observation:

Higher alcohol wines tend to correspond with higher quality ratings.

### Sulphates

Sulphate levels show moderate variation across samples.

### Insight:

Sulphates contribute to wine preservation and stability.

### Correlation Analysis

Important relationships observed include:

Alcohol positively correlates with wine quality
Density negatively correlates with alcohol content
Residual sugar influences density levels
These correlations highlight interactions between chemical properties in wine composition.

### Key Insights

Important findings from the analysis include:

White wines tend to have higher residual sugar levels
Alcohol content positively impacts wine quality
Most wines fall within quality ratings between 6 and 7

### Conclusion

The exploratory analysis provides insights into the chemical characteristics of white wines and the factors that influence wine quality ratings.
Understanding these relationships can help improve wine production processes and quality assessment methods.
